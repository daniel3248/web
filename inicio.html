Comparative: We use comparatives (e.g., "bigger", "faster") to compare two things.

These are used to compare two elements. For example:

  • Tall → Taller: "John is taller than Mark."
  • Beautiful → More beautiful: "This painting is more beautiful than that one."
  • Delicious → more delicious: "There is nothing more delicious than pancakes with caviar."

Level 2: Superlative

In this level, we will test your knowledge of superlative adjectives in English. Remember that comparatives are used to compare two things, while superlatives are used to express the highest degree among three or more items.

Superlatives

We use superlatives (e.g., "biggest", "fastest") to compare three or more things.
  • Tall → Tallest: "John is the tallest in his class."
    • difficult → more difficult
    • Sentence: This test is more difficult than the last one.

    Superlatives are formed using "most" for adjectives with two or more syllables. For example:

    • beautiful → most beautiful
    • Sentence: This is the most beautiful place I have ever seen.
    • Beautiful → The most beautiful: "This is the most beautiful garden I have ever seen."

    Irregular Comparatives and Superlatives

    Some adjectives and adverbs have irregular comparative and superlative forms that you must memorize:

    • Good → Better (comparative) → Best (superlative)
    • Bad → Worse (comparative) → Worst (superlative)
    • Far → Farther (comparative) → Farthest (superlative)

    Additionally, remember to consider context when using comparatives and superlatives for complex sentences.
